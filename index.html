WHY TOKYO?

Tokyo, the world's most populous metropolis

Tokyo is one of Japan's 47 prefectures, consisting of 23 central city wards and multiple cities, towns and villages west of the city center. Prior to 1868, Tokyo was known as Edo, a small castle town, which became Japan's political center and grew into one of the world's largest cities.

Today, Tokyo offers a seemingly unlimited choice of shopping, entertainment, culture and dining to its visitors. The city's history can be appreciated in districts such as Asakusa and in many excellent museums, historic temples and gardens. Contrary to common perception, Tokyo also offers a number of attractive green spaces in the city center and within relatively short train rides at its outskirts.

My favourite places to eat in Tokyo

Mame-Shiba Café

A cafe with miniature shiba-inu dogs.

Address

Shibuya City, Jingumae, 1 Chome−6−10 ３F

What I like about it

The cafe has a homely, traditional atmosphere with tatami flooring and low dining tables, making this experience even more unique, since it feels like playing with these tiny cuties in a regular Japanese home.

Ichiran Ramen

The best ramen in the world

Address

Shinjuku City, Shinjuku, 3 Chome−34−11 B1F

What I like about it

My personal favourite ramen eatery. There are main chains throughout Tokyo and they are very popular. At this chain, you can order and eat your meal without interacting with anyone else, ordering your preference in noodle firmness, spiciness and any extras.

Tokiwa Shokudo

My personal favourite traditional Japanese meal

Address

Toshima City, Sugamo, 3 Chome-14-20

What I like about it

A restaurant very close to me as it was a place I often ate at when I stayed there. It has delicious traditional Japanese set meals which include soup, pickles and rice, as well as tea for very affordable prices.
